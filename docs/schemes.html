Intuition on Schemes

Date: November 2025

We always hear that schemes generalize varieties. But how exactly? And how to we reach this result?
In this post we go through these questions and try to make schemes seem a little more familiar and less daunting. It will be based on Gathmann's notes for reference.
Observation:
By remark 4.16, we have a bijection

$$ \frac{\{\text{affine varieties over $K$}\}}{\cong} \longleftrightarrow \frac{\{\text{finitely generated reduced $K$-algebra}\}}{\cong}, \quad X \mapsto A(X) $$

Given a field $K$, affine varieties correspond to finitely generated reduced $K$-algebras. Therefore, it is very natural to approach a generalization of (affine) varieties be wanting to extend this bijection to all schemes.

Idea

Instead of viewing a variety as the set of points, view it as the set of its irreducible subvarieties. This gives a better perspective on the coordinate ring, since prime ideals correspond to irreducible affine subvarieties.

Reformulations of Affine Varieties

As Sets

Focus on the set $$ X_\text{sch} := \{ Y : Y \text{ affine irreducible subvariety of } X \} $$ rather than $X$ itself.

As Topological Spaces

Define closed subsets of $X_\text{sch}$ as $$ \{ Y : Y \subseteq Y_0, Y \text{ affine irreducible} \} $$ for $Y_0$ affine (not necessarily irreducible). This defines a topology.

As Ringed Spaces

A regular function on an open $U \subseteq X$ is a local rational function: $$ \forall x\in U\ \exists f,g\in A(X), x\in U_x \subseteq U: \forall y \in U_x, f(y)\neq 0, \varphi(y) = \frac{g(y)}{f(y)} $$

On $U_0 = \{ Y : Y \nsubseteq Y_0, Y \text{ irreducible affine}\}$, a function is a collection $(\varphi_Y)_{Y \in U_0}$.

Evaluation of $\varphi$ at $Y$ corresponds to $\varphi_Y \in A(X)_{I(Y)}$, giving rational functions locally on each irreducible affine subvariety.

As Ringed Spaces

Consider $X_\text{sch}$ as the set of prime ideals of $A(X)$: $$ X_\text{sch} := \{\mathfrak{p}: \mathfrak{p} \text{ prime ideal of } A(X)\} = \mathrm{Spec} A(X) $$

For a general ring $R$, define closed sets as $$ V(I) = \{\mathfrak{p}: I \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\} $$ and the structure sheaf: $$ \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Spec} R}(U) = \{ (\varphi_\mathfrak{p})_{\mathfrak{p} \in U}: \forall \mathfrak{p} \in U, \exists f,g \in R, \forall \mathfrak{q} \in U_\mathfrak{p}, f \notin \mathfrak{q}, \varphi_\mathfrak{q} = g/f \in R_\mathfrak{q} \}. $$

Definition:
An affine scheme is a ringed space of the form $\mathrm{Spec} R$ with the above sheaf.
Being over a Field

If $X$ is an affine variety over $K$, then $A(X)$ is a $K$-algebra, giving a scheme morphism $\mathrm{Spec} A(X) \to \mathrm{Spec} K$.
